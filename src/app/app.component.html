Co-Author Network

The co-author network was created from a list of publications. Each author is shown as either a circle or a triangle based on whether they had an affiliation with one of the selected organizations during the time period selected. The size of the author's symbol is based on the number of publications they had during that time period. The color is based on the number of co-authors they had during that time period. Their symbol is labeled if they are one of the selected authors to highlight. Each line represents a co-author relationship where they have published at least one paper together during the selected time period. The thickness of the line is based on the number of publications they authored together during the selected time period.


Science Map

The UCSD map of science and classification system was computed using paper-level data from about 25,000 journals from Elsevier's Scopus and and Thomson Reuters' Web of Science (WoS) for the years 2001-2010. The UCSD map of science assigns the 25,000 journals to 554 subdisciplines that are further aggregated into 13 main disciplines of science. In the map, each discipline has a distinct color (green for 'Biology', brown for 'Earth Sciences', etc.) and a label. (Sub)disciplines that are similar closer to one another on the map. (Sub)disciplines that are especially similar are connected by grey lines.

The details of how this map was created are published in PLOS One. DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0039464
